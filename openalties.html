Play Penalties

The flags come out and
the big play is coming back...

Football is a physical game, but when the game becomes too physical during the play, the official may throw a flag for various penalities. These can be arranged in order of yards. When a penalty would result in a team scoring a touchdown (or in some instances, a safety) the penalty is half the distance to the respective goal line.

Five Yard Penalties: Most five-yard penalties are pre-snap penalties, but here are a few that occur when the ball is live:
-Illegal Foward Pass: Any forward pass that occurs past the line of scrimmage or on a return
-Intentional Grounding: An intentional forward pass thrown where there were no recievers, or to save yardage or time (with the exception of the spike) (if the penalty occurs inside of the offensive five yard line, it results in a safety)
-Ineligble Downfield Reciever: An ineligible reciever is past the line of scrimmage when a forward pass crosses the line
-Illegal Procedure: Either an illegal number of attendants on the field (or unathorised player) during a timeout, a free kick out of bounds or a failure to get within 15 yards of the ball at any time before the snap (some illegal procedure infractions have different penalties that are not yardage based)

Ten Yard Penalties: There are only a few 10 yard penalties, all of which involve illegal usage of the body and hands:
-Illegal Use of the Hands: There are multiple ways to get break this rule, but essentially any grabbing of a teammate, using illegal blocking or defensive technique, or contacting a player illegally with your hands results in this penalty
-Defensive Holding: The usage of hands, arms and legs to lock or clamp a player when that player is not the runner
-Offensive Holding: The usage of hands, arms and legs to lock or clamp a player in an attempt to stop them

Fifteen Yard Penalties: Many 15 yarders are Personal Fouls, but there are a few that don't have the same ugly distinction while carrying the same punishment:
-Illegal Block: While there is no actual Illegal Block penalty, there is illegal block in the back, clipping and chop blocking. All involve an illegal block, usually below the waist, in the back or taking someone out by the knees
-Illegal Batting: Illegally batting the ball forward, attempting to move it towards the end zone or attempting to gain yardage by batting the ball, batting the ball out of the back of the endzone
-Pass Interference: When any player on a team interferes with a player on the other team's ability to move toward or make a play on a ball, when an inelligible reciever is touched by a foward pass before the defense touches it (automatic first down is awarded)
-Fighting: Pretty self explanatory
-Roughing the Passer/Kicker: Hitting or making contact with the passer or kicker after they have released the ball (automatic first down is awarded)
-Illegal Participation: If a subsitute participates in the play in any way, if the offense attempts to decieve the defense, if an injured player does not sit out the necessary down, if a player goes out of bounds and then returns to the field of play
