Personal Fouls

Plays that make you grimace
warrant extra attention...

Some plays are so illegal that they have their own ugly category, personal fouls. We are counting unsportsmanlike conduct as a personal foul because they amount to basically the same thing. A personal foul is a penalty that violates the code of conduct, or involves an excessive and unnecessary amount of violence.

All personal fouls carry a 15 yard penalty, and often the risk of ejection from the game. Usually a personal foul will result in a loss of down. Any kind of fighting, striking or attacking a player is a personal foul. Grabbing an opponent's face mask is another kind of personal foul.

One of the most common personal fouls is unnecessary roughness. This is basically any act of roughness occurring after the play is over. This penalty is often controversial because even if a player's momentum carries him into another player, the penalty is still called. Despite a lack of intent, the late hit is still a personal foul.

Unsportsmanlike conduct may not have the same harsh results as a personal foul, but it does have the same negative connotation. Baiting, taunting, intentionally making a referee's life difficult, excessive celebrations and profanity are all examples of unportsmanlike acts that warrant a 15-yard penalty. In addition, using illegal communication equipment, leaving the sideline as a non-subsitute and other illegal/unfair actions that don't directly influence the field of play are considered unsportsmanlike.
