Game Format

60 minutes to
100 yards...

Football is played on 100 yard field, with 10 yard end zones on both sides of the field. There are markers every 10 yards, with hashmarks to the left and right. These markers are helpful for both officials, players, coaches and fans. At the back of each endzone, there are the field goal posts.

Games are played in four 15-minute quarters. The game starts with a coin toss, where a team can choose to recieve the opening kickoff, defer to the second half, or pick which side of the field they want. In between the first and second quarters, the teams will switch sides (but possession and field position do not change). At the beginning of the second half (no matter where the ball was at the end of the first half) the team that did not recieve the first half kickoff will recieve the second half kickoff. The third and fourth quarter are treated like the first and second quarter.

In the NFL, teams are given three timeouts per half, which they can use at their discretion. In addition, at the two minute remaining mark of both halves, there is a timeout. Before the last two minutes of each half, an injury to a player creates an automatic timeout to take the player off the field. However, inside of the last two minutes, teams will be charged with a timeout for injuries (or a delay of game penalty if they have no timeouts remaining)

NFL coaches can challenge certain rulings on the field (outside of the last two minutes of each half). Teams have two challenges and gain a third if both of the first two are successful. Inside of the last two minutes of the half, replay reviews are initiated from a booth official.
