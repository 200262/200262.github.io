Rules, rules, rules...
football's got lots...

As with any sport, football has rules that are obscure. complicated and often misundetstood. A few of the biggest of those rules will be covered here:

The Catch Rule: The most intensely debated rule in the sport, whether a catch is a catch. While this may seem simple, often times in games you see referees overturn what appears to be a catch or incompletion. To complete the process of a catch, a reciever must maintain possession of the football on their way to the ground while also getting two points of contact in bounds (one in college football). The "Tuck Rule": Made famous by Tom Brady's infamous non-fumble in 2001, the rule on whether a fumble is a fumble or incomplete pass still recieves debate to this day. The NFL rule book defines a foward pass as if the ball moves toward the opponent's goal line initially or hits something (player, ground, official) first at a point closer towards the opponent's goal line. The tricky part comes when the ball moves backwards, but the quarterback is moving his hand forward. The NFL rulebook says that if a player makes a intentional forward motion, the pass is incomplete. In any other situation, it is a fumble.
