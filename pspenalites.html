Pre-Snap Penalites

When the official throws a flag
before the play even starts...

Any penalty that occurs before the snap is considered a pre-snap penalty. These penalities can be called on either the offense or defense and result in a five yard loss. If a team has 12-men on the field when it breaks huddle or snaps the ball then it is either an illegal subsitution or 12-men on the field penalty.

There are a few offensive pre=snap penalties:
-False start: An offensive player moves before the snap (if they are not the designated motion player)
-Illegal formation: A lack of seven men on the line of scrimmage, illegal placement of players behind the line (less than one yard behind), the eligible recievers not being the left and rightmost players on the line, or less than five lineman on the line
-Illegal motion: A man in motion must come out of the backfield and must only move parallel to the line (or be set) before the snap, they cannot be moving towards the line on the snap
-Too many men in motion: More than one man in motion at a time
-Delay of game: When the play clock (40 seconds after a normal play, 25 out of a timeout) expires before the offense snaps the ball

The few defensive pre-snap penalties (encroachment, offside, neutral zone infraction) all involve a defender being on the wrong side of the line of scrimmage. Encroachment involves touching an offensive player while being over the line of scrimmage. Offsides involves being over the line of scrimmage when the ball is snapped. A neutral zone infraction is when a defender's movements over the line coax an offensive player into a false start.
